Macropad Keyboard Extension


Overview: I designed, soldered, and coded a macropad as a keyboard extension for my computer. I modeled the casing in SolidWorks and printed it using a Bambu P1P in PLA, and the keypads were modeled in OnShape and printed in PETG. The plate holding the adafruit tactile keys was laser cut out of acrylic using a Trotec Laser Cutter. I soldered the wires connecting the keys in a matrix formation and soldered them to a Raspberry Pi. All coding was done in Python and uploaded via USB to the Raspberry Pi.

The drawing sheet for the casing is linked here.

The macropad keys shown in the attached picture are emojis, hence the name "Moji Mac." However, the design is modular and can be switched with numeric keypads. I used the numerical keypad configuration for my accounting class as a part of my Business Foundations Minor and will continue to use this for future Business and Engineering classes. This project served as a demonstration of using the skills I've gained in 3D CAD and electronics to create a functional assembly.


Durable Skills Used: Problem Solving, Time Management, Adaptability, Creativity

08/26/2024 - Present


Undergraduate Research


Overview: Working with a team of 5 under my Thermodynamics Professor Dr. Matthew Hall, I have worked on two research projects. For our first project, we were tasked with determining the most efficient running conditions of the UT Engine Group's single-cylinder gasoline engine. Using a National Instruments LabView program, we modified the spark timing, dwell time, air-fuel ratio, fuel injection timing and duration, and the mean effective pressure.

Throughout the research process, we dealt with several issues including the spark plug firing on the wrong edge of the engine stroke, LabView connection failures, the flywheel falling out of callibration, and engine knocking. Equipped with a stroboscopic light, digital osciloscope, encoders, a manometer, and pressure transducer, we were able to troubleshoot these problems using root cause analysis methods. For our first experiment, we changed the duration of the fuel injection, ultimately varying the air-fuel ratio. In our second experiment, we changed the spark control timing using a sweep procedure. Ultimately, we presented our findings at FIRE's annual conference to a group of researchers and engineering professors.

Currently, I am engaged in a research project where we are analyzing the current breakdown event of spark plugs under pressurized conditions to determine degradation mitigation strategies. This project is aiding an ongoing research project led by Gabriel Brito under Dr. Matthew Hall.


Durable Skills Used: Problem Solving, Team Collaboration, Effective Communication, Time Management, Adaptability


Technical Skills Used: Quantitative Analysis, MATLAB, Soldering, Microsoft Suite
